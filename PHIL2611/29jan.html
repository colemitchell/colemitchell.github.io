Chapter 2: The Traditional Analysis of Knowledge
Tue., Jan. 29
In this class, we discuss Chapter 2 of the Feldman textbook. §I covers different types of knowledge and puts our focus on propositional knowledge. §II presents two widely-accepted conditions on knowledge—truth and belief—and explains why a further condition seems to be needed. §III examines the traditional analysis of knowledge as true justified belief (JTB), spending a good deal of time on the so-called correspondence theory of truth.
Key Concepts: Propositional Knowledge, JTB, Correspondence Theory of Truth
