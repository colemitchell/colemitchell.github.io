Cole Mitchell

  • I work in metaethics, ethical theory, early modern, and the history of ethics and metaethics. I also have interests in philosophy of religion, philosophy of mind, applied ethics, and most things historical. See:
    • my CV
    • my 2017 Philosophical Studies article
    • my dissertation
  • I’m now precepting for Princeton University’s PHI 202 (Introduction to Moral Philosophy). In August 2011, I got my Ph.D. with a dissertation on moral semantics and ontology at the University of Arizona. For the 2011–12 school year, I worked as an Instructor for Northern Arizona University. From 2012–14, I was a Visiting Assistant Professor with the University of North Carolina philosophy department, in the PPE program. In Spring 2019, I taught at Cornell University as a Visiting Assistant Professor.

    • online version of King’s Origin of Evil (not quite finished)
    • online guide to the moral realism debate (very incomplete)
    • online guide to texts in early modern metaethics
    • summary of Hume’s Treatise (finished)
