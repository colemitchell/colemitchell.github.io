Leviathan - Day 2
Wed., Aug. 27
In this class, we continue with Leviathan, discussing some fairly scattered chapters. Chapter VI presents both Hobbes’s account of the passions (touching on questions of egoism) as well as his account of voluntary action (touching on questions of free will). Chapters VII, VIII, and IX resume the psychologically-oriented discussion of science: drawing conclusions; opinion, faith, and belief; natural wit and madness; the various branches of science. Chapter X sheds light on Hobbes’s picture of human interaction, giving a very broad account of what power and honor can consist in. And Chapter XI begins to introduce the topic of violence, attributing a fundamental desire for power to human nature, and sketching particular factors that explain cooperative or dangerous behavior.
