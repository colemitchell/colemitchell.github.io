Leviathan - Day 1
Mon., Aug. 25
In this class, we examine the beginning of Hobbes’s masterpiece Leviathan. The three main topics are: (1) The Introduction’s account of a commonwealth as an artificial human, and recommendation to study human nature via self-examination. (2) The first three chapters’ account of the natural faculties of the human mind: sense, imagination, and trains of thoughts. (3) Chapters IV and V on how language enables humans to develop distinctive advanced faculties of reasoning and science. We also give some attention to his repeated potshots at religion, and the question of how all this relates to politics.
