Intro to Descartes
Thu., Jan. 16
This class session covers four topics: (1) The course syllabus; (2) Philosophy and the mind-body problem; (3) Descartes in general; (4) Descartes’ 1637 work Discourse on the Method on mind-body dualism and the distinction between animal souls and human souls. Descartes was a rationalist who argued that pure intellectual thought could reveal not only important mathematical truths, but also the metaphysical and physical structure of our universe. He held that each human being is composed of a body (extended substance) and a mind (thinking substance), two substances that are really distinct from each other (despite being somehow joined together into a composite substance). Animals, on the other hand, are simply part of the physical universe, indistinguishable from (highly sophisticated) machines or automatons.
Key Concepts: Metaphysics, Rationalism, The Mind-Body Problem, Dualism
