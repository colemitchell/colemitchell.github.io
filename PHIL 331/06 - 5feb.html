Locke on thinking matter
Wed., Feb. 5
This class session covers John Locke’s infamous suggestion that maybe God could make certain kinds of matter (“some System of Matter fitly disposed”) capable of thinking. We also touch on his distinction between primary and secondary qualities, and his skepticism about ever understanding the way the former qualities produce ideas of the latter qualities in our mind.
Key Concepts: Thinking Matter, Superaddition, Primary and Secondary Qualities
