Descartes and his critics
Mon., Jan. 27
This class session covers the major criticisms of Descartes’ views and arguments from his contemporaries. We focus mainly on objections from Antoine Arnauld and Princess Elisabeth of Bohemia: Arnauld posed a serious objection to Descartes’ Sixth Meditation ‘conceivability argument’, and Elisabeth posed a serious objection to Descartes’ mind-body interactionism. Descartes tried his best to counter both objections.
Key Concepts: Adequate and Complete Understanding, The Interaction Problem
