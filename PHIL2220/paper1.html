  • Citation practices: Honestly, don’t worry too much about this, but if you want to do it just right, here is a link to the standard edition of Meditations. The different texts occur in different volumes, as illustrated by the following three examples:
    • In the Meditations, Descartes writes of a “great light in the intellect” (CSM 2:41). [In other words, volume 2 for the Meditations and page 41.]
    • In the Principles, Descartes uses the phrase “if we could not do otherwise” in contrast with “our [embracing the truth] voluntarily” (CSM 1:205). [In other words, volume 1 for the Principles and page 205.]
    • In a letter to Elisabeth, Descartes writes that God is “the first and immutable cause of all effects that do not depend on human free will” as well as “all the effects that do so depend” (CSMK 3:272). [In other words, volume 3 for the correspondence and page 272. Note that volume 3 is abbreviated ‘CSMK’ and not just ‘CSM’.]
