Ethics: rationalist backlash
Thu., Apr. 17
In this class, we discuss the rationalist backlash against voluntarism and egoism. First, we examine some of the objections against radical divine command theory from such theorists as Cudworth, Leibniz, and Samuel Clarke—especially the circle of authority and obligation. Second, we examine Bishop Joseph Butler’s critique of egoism, in which he argues that not all motives are self-interested and that self-love requires disinterested affections in order to pursue its goal of happiness.
Key Concepts: Intellectualism, Rationalism, Obligation, Egoism, Paradox of Egoism
