English Platonists: More, Cudworth, Conway
Tue., Feb. 11
In this class, we cover Platonist reactions to the mechanical philosophy of Descartes and Hobbes. In these excerpts, we see the following: Henry More attempts to counter Hobbes’s dogmatic rejection of incorporeal substances with an account of the soul as an extended substance. Ralph Cudworth argues for “Plastick natures” that can explain how unintelligent natural things behave intelligently while striking just the right theological balance. Anne Conway argues for a monist metaphysics with no essential distinction between mind and body: the created universe consists of spirits and bodies that only differ in degree of spirituality, so that a spirit can change into a body and a body can change into a spirit.
