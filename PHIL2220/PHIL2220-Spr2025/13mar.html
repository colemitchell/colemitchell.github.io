Locke’s Essay concerning Human Understanding
Thu., Mar. 13
In this class, we continue discussing Locke’s Essay. We first focus on the inadequacies in our idea of substance, and the distinction between real essence and nominal essence. We then focus on two concrete cases: personal identity and the possibility of thinking matter. In both cases, Locke argues that it doesn’t matter much for practical/theological purposes whether our thinking self can be identified with a single enduring spiritual substance For personal identity, what matters is a certain continued consciousness, regardless of the underlying substance. For thinking matter, what matters is that God has somehow established some sort of relation between motions in our body and our thoughts, regardless of how close that relation is. In any case, my consciousness can be present at the Last Judgment, and I can be held accountable for all that I’ve done.
Key Concepts: Substance, Real Essence, Personal Identity, Thinking Matter
