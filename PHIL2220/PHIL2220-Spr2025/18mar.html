Berkeley’s Treatise concerning the Principles of Human Knowledge
Tue., Mar. 18
In this class, we begin discussing Berkeley’s Principles. According to Berkeley, common sense has no use for the mysterious philosophical doctrine of material substance with absolute existence, and careful examination reveals the doctrine to be flatly impossible. In Berkeley'’s system, only spirits and ideas exist, so that just as a pain depends for its existence on being perceived, the entire natural world depends for its existence on being perceived by spirits. Berkeley rejects abstract ideas by which we would conceive of impossibilities, and he thinks the attempt to conceive of something existing unperceived is just another instance of the noxious influence of the doctrine of abstract ideas.
Key Concepts: Immaterialism, Idealism
