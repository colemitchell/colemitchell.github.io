Spinoza’s Ethics I
Fri., Feb. 15
In this class, we begin discussing the metaphysical system in Spinoza’s Ethics. Heavily influenced by Descartes and Hobbes, Spinoza took modern philosophy in a very radical direction. In Part I, he argues that all reality consists in one infinite substance (‘God’ or ‘Nature’), which has no human-like attributes (no supreme intellect or free will or higher purposes or concern for human well-being), which couldn’t have been any different than it actually is, and from which everything that happens follows with strict necessity. This substance has infinitely many attributes, but we can understand only two of them: Thought and Extension. From the substance and its attributes there follow infinite psychological modes, infinite physical modes, finite psychological modes (minds), and finite physical modes (bodies).
Key Concepts: Substance Monism, Pantheism and Atheism, Necessity
