English Platonists: More, Conway
Tue., Feb. 10
In this class, we cover Platonist reactions to the mechanical philosophy of Descartes and Hobbes. In these excerpts, we see the following: Henry More attempts to counter Hobbes’s dogmatic rejection of incorporeal substances with a non-Cartesian form of dualism that treats the soul as an extended substance. Anne Conway argues for a monist metaphysics with no essential distinction between mind and body: the created universe consists of spirits and bodies that only differ in degree of spirituality, so that a spirit can change into a body and a body can change into a spirit.
