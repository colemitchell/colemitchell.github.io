Berkeley’s Treatise concerning the Principles of Human Knowledge
Wed., Mar. 6
In this class, we finish discussing Berkeley’s Principles. According to Berkeley, common sense has no use for the mysterious philosophical doctrine of material substance with absolute existence, and careful examination reveals the doctrine to be flatly impossible. In Berkeley's system, only spirits and ideas exist, so that just as a pain depends for its existence on being perceived, the entire natural world depends for its existence on being perceived.
Key Concepts: Immaterialism, Idealism
