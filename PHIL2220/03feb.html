Sixth Meditation, Descartes’ Principles, Elisabeth of Bohemia
Tue., Feb. 3
In this class, we finish up on the Fifth Meditation (“The essence of material things, and the existence of God considered a second time”), and begin discussing the Sixth Meditation (“The existence of material things, and the real distinction between mind and body”). Descartes finally presents his argument for the existence of a material world, argues that he has an completely immaterial soul (closely united with, but really distinct from, a body), and discusses how far nature can be trusted. In an excerpt from his textbook project The Principles of Philosophy, we see Descartes’ substance/attribute/mode metaphysics and his theory of distinctions. In the correspondence with Elisabeth of Bohemia, we see how Descartes attempts to respond to a tough objection about how mind and body could possibly interact.
Key Concepts: Imagination vs. Intellect, Cartesian Dualism, Theory of Distinction
