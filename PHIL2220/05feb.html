English materialists: Hobbes, Cavendish
Thu., Feb. 5
In this class, we take a brief look at a couple of English materialists who develop their views partly in reaction against Descartes’ combination of mechanical physics and mind-body dualism. Hobbes takes a straightforward and uncom­promising approach on which being real means being extended and therefore being corporeal (so that even spirits, angels, and God would need to be corporeal), whereas Cavendish (represented here in an excerpt from her Obser­vations upon Experimental Philosophy) takes a more unconventional approach that in some sense attributes life and mentality to all matter in order to explain the order found in nature.
