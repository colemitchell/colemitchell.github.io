Ethics: voluntarism and egoism
Tue., Apr. 15
In this class, we discuss two approaches to ethics often lumped together in the early modern period: voluntarism and egoism. First, we examine the Euthyphro dilemma between rationalism/intellectualism and divine command theory. Descartes’ doctrine of the creation of the eternal truths, together with Hobbes’s account of God’s right to rule in terms of irresistible power, are our examples of radical voluntarism. Next, we discuss Hobbes’s self-preservation–based “moral philosophy”—his account of the laws of nature—and Bernard Mandeville’s account of our ideas of virtue and vice in terms of skillful politicians using flattery to appeal to our pride.
Key Concepts: Euthyphro Dilemma, Voluntarism, Egoism
