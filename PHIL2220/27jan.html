Third Meditation
Tue., Jan. 27
In this class, we begin discussing the Third Meditation, entitled “The existence of God”. Descartes first extracts a promising general rule from the cogito: any clear and distinct perception must be true. He then presents a new spin on an old demonstration of the existence of God: instead of arguing that the universe is so wonderful that it must have an infinitely perfect divine cause, he argues that the idea of God in his mind has so much ‘objective reality’ that it must have an infinitely perfect divine cause.
Key Concepts: Clear and Distinct Perceptions, Innate Ideas, Formal and Objective Reality, Causation, God
