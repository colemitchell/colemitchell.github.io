Wollstonecraft’s Vindication
Tue., May 6
In this class, we discuss Wollstonecraft’s A Vindication of the Rights of Woman. Wollstonecraft argues that women are in a degraded condition because of the way they are educated (where education is understood broadly to include all sorts of early social influences in a girl’s environment. Wollstonecraft argues for a unified account of virtue that doesn’t admit of fundamentally distinct virtues for men and women. Instead, she draws on a rationalist approach to morality and the presupposition that every human has a soul created for a personal immortality.
Key Concepts: Reason, Virtue, Education, Sex, Gender
